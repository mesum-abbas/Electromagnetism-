Faraday's Law of Induction
1831 - Foundations of Electromagnetism

Faraday's law states that a changing magnetic flux through a circuit induces an electromotive force (EMF). In simple terms, moving a magnet near a coil, or changing the current in a nearby coil, creates a voltage in the circuit. The induced EMF is proportional to how quickly the flux changes, which is the core principle behind generators and transformers.

Michael Faraday was a British scientist who pioneered experimental electromagnetism and electrochemistry. His 1831 experiments demonstrated electromagnetic induction, laying the groundwork for modern electric power.
